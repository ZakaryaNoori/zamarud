Lapis Lazuli Facts

Lapis Lazuli is among the rarest semi-precious stones.
That the earth has to offer - And they’re rare for a reason.

For the entire history stretching across thousands of years,
Afghanistan has remained the epicenter for mining of the most
exquisite and royal lapis lazuli stones.

What Lapis Lazuli Are and Are not

Afghanistan has been one of the world's primary sources of lapis lazuli through most of recorded history. Most of the country's production comes from thousands of small mines in the Badakhshan Province. This is an area with a destitute economy, where gemstones mining are the only important sources of outside revenue. Mining for lapis occurred in the Badakhshan Province of northeastern Afghanistan as early as 7000 BC. The lapis was used to make beads, small jewelry items and small sculptures.

Even today Afghanistan accounts for a major share of highest quality lapis production. Other areas of mining for lapis include Pakistan, Russia, Chile, Canada, Angola, and US. However, the stones extracted from the mines of Afghanistan are still valued above others because of their purity, splendor, and magnificence.

What Makes an Lapis Lazuli Valuable?

The most highly prized lapis lazuli stones are uniformly colored and without a violet tinge. Lapis lazuli stones vary in hardness, ranging from a 5 to 5.5 on the hardness scale. The stone has vitreous luster.

Lapis Lazuli is found as boulders within limestone. It has been mined in Afghanistan since remote antiquity. In fact, ancient Egyptians probably obtained their supplies from there. Argentina is also a source of lapis lazuli but Afghanistan is the best high quality stones mines.

Here’s what you should know about this exquisite stone.

  • It is one of the oldest gemstone that has been collected for over 6000 years
  • The name Lapis Lazuli literally translate into Blue Stone, which represents the magnificent color of the stone
  • A magical stone that derives its name “lapis lazuli” from two words of different origin, “Lapis” is of Latin origin, which means “Stone” whereas, “Azula” comes from Arabic origin meaning “Blue”.
  • It’s basically a rock and not a mineral
  • Lazurite – a blue stone, is primarily responsible for the color of the stone
  • It’s a semi-precious stone
